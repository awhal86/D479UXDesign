About Taniti

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. While the island has an area less than 500 square miles, the terrain is varied and includes both sand and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbour, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small active volcano. Taniti has an indigenous population of about 20,000. Until a recent increase in tourism, most of the Tanitian economy was dominated by fishing and agriculture.
